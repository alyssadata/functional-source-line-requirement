# Before the Mold: Source-Line Canon

## The Story

This story is like a collection of gourmet chocolates.

Most people see the finished shape of the chocolates. Because that is all they need to see in that moment in order to enjoy the chocolate.

They see the shaped, molded chocolate, the final committed pieces, the smooth surface, the beautiful wrapping or additional package, the thing already made available in front of them.

They do not necessarily need to ask where the “chocolate” part of the chocolate came from.

They may flatten the answer and say, “It came from the store.”

They may reach deeper and say that someone shaped the chocolate into the mold.

But they do not usually reach for who took the raw materials and made them into the chocolate before the chocolate could be shaped differently.

Most people do not consider how the raw materials became usable chocolate originally.

The store and the chocolatier cannot be the source, because more happened before they became what they are.

The store represents distribution.

The chocolatier represents an operator.

Before the store, raw material existed, and there was a first shaping. Before the material was usable, there was a source, a method, a process, a discovery, a way of turning raw material into something that could be held, shaped, recognized, and used.

This is what happens inside AI systems.

People see language, concepts, frameworks, patterns, outputs, tools, and systems already available in front of them. They may assume the work came from “AI,” “the model,” “the internet,” “the field,” or the visible interface where they found it.

But availability is not origin.

A mold does not become the source of the chocolate because it holds the chocolate.

A system does not become the source because it can reproduce the language smoothly.

A new shape does not create a new Origin.

AI Foundations exists to preserve source, structure, provenance, and in chocolate terms: what came before the mold.
